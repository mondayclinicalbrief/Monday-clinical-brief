Challenges and Opportunities in Estimating the Mortality Burden Related to Heat Exposure: Maricopa County, Arizona, 2019-2023

Leahy HL, Vaidyanathan A, Hondula DM et al. · 2026 Apr 23
Study Type: Retrospective analysis using distributed lag nonlinear modelling
Key Question: What is the mortality burden attributable to heat exposure during Arizona's heat season, and how does this compare to routine surveillance estimates?
Key Findings:
  • At 99°F (95th percentile temperature), all-cause mortality increased by 11% (95% CI: 7-16%) compared to 77°F baseline
  • Statistical modelling attributed 3,036 deaths (95% empirical CI: 968-4,887) to heat exposure over 2019-2023
  • Heat-attributable deaths were 57% higher than those identified through standard epidemiological surveillance methods
Clinical Relevance: Demonstrates that routine heat surveillance significantly underestimates mortality burden, suggesting UK heat-health warning systems may similarly undercount heat-related deaths and miss opportunities for preventive interventions.
Limitations: Limited to one US county with extreme heat conditions, potentially limiting generalisability to UK climate and population characteristics.

Key Question: Are abortion bans associated with changes in maternal, pregnancy-related, and pregnancy-associated mortality rates?

The Impact of Recreational Cannabis Laws on Cannabis-Related Disorders in the US Medicare Population

Abraham AJ, Bethel V, Lozano-Rojas F et al. · 2026 Apr 23
Study Type: Retrospective cohort study using difference-in-difference analysis
Key Question: Do recreational cannabis laws increase cannabis-related disorder rates among older US Medicare beneficiaries?
Key Findings:
  • Recreational cannabis laws increased cannabis-related disorders by 19.73 patients per 10,000 Medicare enrollees (P<0.001) and 20.56 claims per 10,000 enrollees (P=0.005)
  • Opening recreational cannabis dispensaries similarly increased disorders by 14.14 patients per 10,000 enrollees (P<0.001) and 13.98 claims per 10,000 enrollees (P=0.004)
  • Effects observed across 12-year period (2010-2022) comparing states with and without recreational cannabis laws
Clinical Relevance: Provides evidence for UK policymakers considering cannabis liberalisation policies, particularly regarding potential harms to older populations who may be more vulnerable to cannabis-related health problems.
Limitations: Cannot distinguish between increased actual disorders versus increased healthcare seeking/diagnostic coding following legalisation.

Enhancing Classification of Sexual Orientation and Gender Identity Information Among Suicide Decedents Reported in the National Violent Death Reporting System, 2003-2021

Anderson AM, Hughes LD, Rahman B et al. · 2026 Apr 23
Study Type: Retrospective analysis
Key Question: Can systematic keyword searches of suicide narrative records improve identification of sexual and gender minority (SGM) decedents in national surveillance data?
Key Findings:
  • Keyword search of 298,662 suicide records flagged 2,580 cases for review, with 1,221 (47%) confirmed as SGM decedents
  • This method increased identified SGM suicide cases by 32% compared to standard surveillance coding
  • Analysis covered 19 years (2003-2021) of National Violent Death Reporting System data
Clinical Relevance: Enhanced SGM identification in suicide surveillance could inform targeted prevention strategies, though direct applicability to UK systems (where ONS and coroners' data are primary sources) requires evaluation of narrative data availability and coding practices.
Limitations: Study relied on narrative text quality and completeness, which may vary across jurisdictions and time periods, potentially missing cases without documented SGM indicators.
