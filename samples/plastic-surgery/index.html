Effect of Botulinum Toxin Injection Intervals on the Irreversibility of Targeted Muscle Paralysis: An Electromyographic Study

Ahmed MB, Al Aloosi M, Cohen JA et al. · 2026 Apr 21
Study Type: Randomized controlled trial
Key Question: Does prolonged short-interval botulinum toxin A administration to glabellar muscles induce irreversible or semi-permanent paralysis as measured by electromyography?
Key Findings:
  • 21 women received BoNT-A injections (20U) at 6-, 9-, or 12-week intervals for 108 weeks, with 48-week follow-up
  • All groups showed sustained EMG suppression that persisted 48 weeks after treatment cessation, remaining well below baseline levels
  • Shorter injection intervals (6-9 weeks) demonstrated greater sustained chemodenervation effect compared to 12-week intervals
Clinical Relevance: This suggests that frequent BoNT-A treatments may produce longer-lasting effects than traditional 3-4 month intervals, potentially reducing treatment frequency and costs for aesthetic patients in UK practice.
Limitations: Small sample size (21 patients) limits generalizability and requires larger studies with longer follow-up to confirm clinical significance of these electrophysiological findings.

Piezoelectric Versus Conventional Rhinoplasty: A GRADE-assessed Systematic Review and Meta-analysis of Randomized Controlled Trials

Armanfar S, Ardakani MR, Vahidiataabadi M · 2026 Apr 21
Study Type: Systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials
Key Question: Does piezoelectric osteotomy reduce postoperative morbidity compared to conventional osteotomy techniques in rhinoplasty?
Key Findings:
  • Piezoelectric technique significantly reduced oedema at postoperative days 2 and 7 (SMD -0.92 and -0.64 respectively), ecchymosis at days 1, 2, and 7 (SMD ranging from -0.62 to -1.33), and pain at day 2 (SMD -1.23)
  • Mucosal integrity loss occurred in zero piezoelectric cases versus controls (RR 0.09, 95% CI 0.02-0.44)
  • Operative time showed no significant difference but with considerable heterogeneity between studies
Clinical Relevance: This evidence supports piezoelectric osteotomy as a technique to improve patient experience and reduce early complications in rhinoplasty, potentially reducing recovery time and enhancing surgical outcomes in NHS practice.
Limitations: Considerable heterogeneity in operative time outcomes suggests variability in technique implementation and measurement across studies.

Orbicularis Oculi Toxin Injection for Treating Levator Palpebrae Superioris Weakness: Clinical Effect and Safety in a Cohort Study

Lu S, Wei Q, Ma J et al. · 2026 Apr 23
Study Type: Cohort study
Key Question: Is botulinum toxin injection into the orbicularis oculi muscle effective and safe compared to levator shortening surgery for treating ptosis?
Key Findings:
  • Botulinum toxin group achieved greater improvement in palpebral fissure height (3.5mm vs 1.7mm in surgical controls)
  • Higher patient satisfaction in toxin group due to non-invasive approach and shorter recovery
  • Lower adverse event rate with fewer complications such as scarring compared to surgery
Clinical Relevance: Offers UK plastic surgeons a potential non-surgical alternative for selected ptosis patients, particularly relevant given NHS emphasis on minimally invasive treatments and reduced surgical waiting lists.
Limitations: Small sample size (36 patients) and no mention of long-term follow-up data to assess durability of treatment effects.

Longitudinal Computed Tomographic Evaluation of Mandibular Bone Morphology Following OnabotulinumtoxinA Treatment of Masseter Muscle Prominence: Results of a 12-Month, Repeat-Treatment, Placebo-Controlled Study in Healthy Adults

Mallya SM, Kostenuik P, Hopfinger R et al. · 2026 Apr 24
Study Type: Randomized controlled trial
Key Question: Does onabotulinumtoxinA treatment for masseter muscle prominence cause mandibular bone morphological changes over 12 months?
Key Findings:
  • No significant differences in quantitative mandibular measurements (bigonial width, cortical thickness, mandibular flare, gonial angles) between onabotulinumtoxinA (n=150) and placebo (n=37) groups
  • 94% of participants showed no qualitative changes to mandibular condylar head, glenoid fossa, or articular eminence on CT assessment
  • Most common treatment-related adverse event was mastication disorder (6.0%), consistent with expected mechanism of action
Clinical Relevance: This addresses safety concerns about off-label botulinum toxin use for facial contouring, providing evidence-based reassurance for UK practitioners considering or currently offering this aesthetic treatment.
Limitations: Study duration limited to 12 months, so longer-term skeletal effects beyond one year remain unknown.

Key Question: Can in-house 3D-printed digital nasal stents provide an economical alternative to nasoalveolar molding (NAM) for presurgical nasal correction in cleft lip patients?
Key Findings:
  • Custom CAD/CAM digital nasal stents fabricated using resin-based 3D printing successfully improved cleft nasal deformity before primary reconstruction
  • Concurrent lip taping achieved simultaneous alveolar gap reduction

Unusual Vascular Anomalies in Pediatric Hand and Plastic Surgery: A Case Series

Sharma SC, Jester A, Vidyadharan R · 2026 Apr 22
Study Type: Case series
Key Question: How should plastic surgeons approach diagnosis and management of rare pediatric vascular anomalies that present diagnostic and therapeutic challenges?
Key Findings:
  • Five complex cases included kaposiform hemangioendothelioma with Kasabach-Merritt phenomenon, congenital hemangioma, generalized lymphatic anomaly, and PIK3CA-related overgrowth spectrum disorder with osteolytic lesions
  • Multi-modal treatment combining surgical reconstruction with targeted systemic therapies (propranolol, sirolimus, alpelisib, corticosteroids) achieved significant symptomatic and functional improvement with good tolerance
  • Diagnosis required integration of radiological, histopathological, and genetic/molecular testing
Clinical Relevance: UK plastic surgeons managing pediatric vascular anomalies need awareness of these rare conditions and their mimicry of common pathology, plus familiarity with novel targeted therapies within MDT frameworks.
Limitations: Small case series from single tertiary centre limits generalisability of management approaches and outcomes.

Lymph-interpositional flap transfer promotes lymphatic flow restoration: A rabbit hindlimb model supports the lymph axiality concept

Lee J, Lee SB, Chung JH et al. · 2026 Apr 08
Study Type: Experimental animal study (rabbit model)
Key Question: Does lymph-interpositional flap transfer (LIFT) with maintained lymphatic alignment improve lymphatic regeneration compared to misaligned reconstruction?
Key Findings:
  • LIFT with maintained alignment showed significantly reduced limb volume at 3 weeks (p=0.02) and higher lymphatic reconnection rates on ICG lymphography (p=0.021)
  • Molecular analysis revealed elevated lymphangiogenic markers (VEGF-C, CD31, podoplanin) and reduced fibrotic markers (TGF-β) in aligned reconstruction (all p<0.05)
  • Volumetric differences disappeared by 6 weeks, suggesting early benefit of the technique
Clinical Relevance: This provides experimental evidence for LIFT technique in lymphatic reconstruction, potentially informing surgical planning for lymphoedema patients treated in NHS plastic surgery services.
Limitations: Animal model findings may not translate directly to human lymphatic reconstruction outcomes.

Outcomes of cosmetic suturing versus tissue adhesive for pediatric forehead lacerations: A retrospective cohort study adjusting for injury severity

Zhang Y, Cui Y, Li F et al. · 2026 Apr 09
Study Type: Retrospective cohort study
Key Question: Does cosmetic layered suturing versus tissue adhesive affect dynamic expression dimpling and scar quality in paediatric forehead lacerations?
Key Findings:
  • Tissue adhesive was associated with quadrupled risk of dynamic expression dimpling compared to suturing (11.5% vs 3.0%, OR=3.92, P=0.029) despite treating less severe wounds
  • Long-term scar appearance at 12 months was determined by initial wound depth and postoperative laser therapy, not closure method
  • Significant selection bias existed with suture group managing deeper and longer wounds
Clinical Relevance: This challenges the widespread use of tissue adhesives for paediatric facial lacerations in UK emergency departments, suggesting layered suturing may be necessary to prevent functional contour deformities in forehead injuries.
Limitations: Retrospective design with significant selection bias between treatment groups, despite propensity score matching attempts.
