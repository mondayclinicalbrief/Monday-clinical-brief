A Novel Pulsatile Excised Porcine Lung Model for Realistic Anatomic Lung Resection Training: A Pilot Study

Kawamura T, Yanagihara T, Sugai K et al. · 2026 Apr 01
Study Type: Pilot study
Key Question: Can a novel pulsatile porcine lung model with microbead-filled pulmonary arteries provide realistic anatomic lung resection training without pulmonary oedema?
Key Findings:
  • Microbead infusion reduced pulmonary oedema significantly (mean weight gain 16.4g vs 457.0g in controls, P=0.027)
  • Eight trainees showed significant improvement in lobectomy time from first to second attempt (39 min 17s to 32 min 52s, P=0.012)
  • 75% rated vascular manipulation as realistic; 100% found training beneficial
Clinical Relevance: This training model could enhance surgical education for UK cardiothoracic trainees by providing realistic pulsatile vessel simulation for lobectomy and vascular repair skills development.
Limitations: Very small pilot study with only 8 trainees limits generalisability of findings.

Beyond annuloplasty: Anatomy-based tricuspid valve repair for right ventricular dysfunction

Arai H, Grau JB · 2026 Apr 22
Study Type: Narrative review
Key Question: What advanced tricuspid valve repair techniques beyond annuloplasty are available for managing functional tricuspid regurgitation with right ventricular dysfunction?
Key Findings:
  • Review consolidates surgical techniques including edge-to-edge repair, leaflet augmentation, papillary muscle procedures, and annular repositioning for complex tricuspid pathology
  • Emphasises that successful repair requires addressing the interplay between annular, leaflet, and subvalvular distortion in RV dilatation
  • Proposes these techniques as a comprehensive surgical armamentarium to restore normal leaflet geometry and achieve durable results
Clinical Relevance: Provides UK cardiothoracic surgeons with a structured framework for managing complex tricuspid regurgitation, particularly relevant as transcatheter tricuspid interventions become more prevalent and surgical standards require refinement.
Limitations: As a narrative review, lacks systematic methodology and quantitative outcome data to guide technique selection.

Corynebacterium striatum infection in incision and thoracic cavity with concurrent CRKP colonization following lung and bladder tumor resection

Li X, Liao L · 2026 Apr 20
Study Type: Case report
Key Question: How should clinicians manage postoperative thoracic infections when Corynebacterium striatum is identified alongside multidrug-resistant organisms like CRKP?
Key Findings:
  • C. striatum caused true postoperative thoracic cavity infection (confirmed by pleural fluid culture) while CRKP represented pulmonary colonization only
  • Targeted vancomycin therapy successfully treated the C. striatum infection despite concurrent CRKP presence
  • Clinical pharmacy consultation was essential in differentiating pathogen from coloniser and selecting appropriate antimicrobial therapy
Clinical Relevance: UK cardiothoracic surgeons should consider C. striatum as a potential pathogen in post-operative infections, particularly given increasing antimicrobial resistance and the need for targeted therapy to avoid unnecessary broad-spectrum antibiotic use in line with NHS antimicrobial stewardship priorities.
Limitations: Single case report limits generalisability of management approach.

Key Question: How should extrinsic coronary compression by left ventricular pseudoaneurysm be managed following endocarditis surgery?
Key Findings:
  • Patient developed acute MI from left main stem compression by pseudoaneurysm formation under septal abscess patch repair
  • PET imaging confirmed acute LV hypoperfusion necessitating immediate high-risk reoperation

Mechanical versus bioprosthetic conduits for the bentall procedure: a 10-year propensity score-matched analysis of early and long-term outcomes

Eroglu Polat EA, Altaylı G, Donbaloglu MO et al. · 2026 Apr 22
Study Type: Retrospective cohort study with propensity score matching
Key Question: Do mechanical versus bioprosthetic conduits differ in early and long-term outcomes following Bentall procedures?
Key Findings:
  • No significant difference in long-term overall survival or reoperation-free survival between mechanical and bioprosthetic conduits at median 36-month follow-up
  • Bioprosthetic conduits associated with significantly higher early re-exploration for bleeding (24.7% vs 6.5%, OR 4.72, 95% CI 1.66-13.40)
  • Early mortality was comparable between groups despite increased bleeding risk with bioprosthetics
Clinical Relevance: This challenges traditional assumptions about bioprosthetic durability in Bentall procedures, suggesting both conduit types offer equivalent mid-term outcomes for UK surgeons, though bioprosthetics require enhanced hemostatic technique.
Limitations: Relatively short median follow-up of 36 months limits assessment of long-term bioprosthetic durability traditionally expected beyond 10-15 years.

Bacillus Calmette-Guérin related mycotic pseudoaneurysm in the aortic arch: clinical and surgical challenges

Bonni S, Ramnath NWM, Selten K et al. · 2026 Apr 24
Study Type: Case report
Key Question: How should clinicians manage mycotic pseudoaneurysms of the aortic arch secondary to intravesical BCG therapy?
Key Findings:
  • A patient developed aortic arch pseudoaneurysm following BCG treatment for bladder cancer, confirmed as Mycobacterium bovis infection on histopathology
  • Treatment required aortic arch and descending aorta replacement with antimicrobial therapy
  • Patient died one month postoperatively from stroke and multi-organ failure despite surgical intervention
Clinical Relevance: UK cardiothoracic surgeons should maintain high suspicion for mycotic aneurysms in patients with previous BCG therapy presenting with systemic symptoms, as these represent surgical emergencies requiring urgent multidisciplinary care.
Limitations: Single case report limits generalisability of management approach and outcomes.

Health-related quality of life and associated factors before and after coronary artery bypass grafting: A multicenter prospective study in cyprus

Alexandrou K, Middleton N, Kyranou M et al. · 2026 Apr 24
Study Type: Prospective multicentre cohort study
Key Question: How does health-related quality of life change after coronary artery bypass grafting and what factors influence these changes?
Key Findings:
  • HRQOL scores declined at 3 months post-CABG (5.03±1.02 vs 4.75±1.00, p=0.015) but improved significantly by 6 months (5.99±0.67), exceeding preoperative levels
  • Male patients had consistently higher HRQOL scores at both 3- and 6-month follow-up (p=0.011 and p=0.009 respectively)
  • Age, postoperative arrhythmias, left ventricular ejection fraction, and surgical approach did not significantly influence HRQOL outcomes
Clinical Relevance: This provides evidence for UK cardiothoracic teams that patients should expect temporary quality of life deterioration post-CABG before significant improvement, supporting informed consent discussions and postoperative counselling strategies.
Limitations: Single-country study from Cyprus may limit generalisability to UK NHS patient populations with different demographics and healthcare systems.

The Annals of thoracic surgery

Impact of preoperative left ventricular dimensions on early outcomes after mitral valve repair for degenerative mitral regurgitation

Wang A, Gammie JS, Mackay SD et al. · 2026 Apr 17
Study Type: Retrospective single-centre cohort study
Key Question: Does preoperative left ventricular end-diastolic diameter (LVEDD) better predict early outcomes than left ventricular end-systolic diameter (LVESD) in patients undergoing mitral valve repair for degenerative mitral regurgitation?
Key Findings:
  • Only 1.4% of patients met Class I operative indications based on LVESD ≥4cm, whilst 35% had elevated sex-specific LVEDD thresholds
  • Preoperative LVEDD independently predicted 30-day readmission (OR 3.19, 95% CI 1.56-6.70) and new-onset atrial fibrillation (OR 2.41, 95% CI 1.03-5.73), whereas LVESD did not
  • Among Class IIa patients with elevated LVEDD, 25% had lower discharge LVEF and higher cardiac-related readmissions
Clinical Relevance: This challenges current guideline reliance on LVESD thresholds and suggests LVEDD may be a more clinically useful marker for timing mitral valve intervention in UK practice.
Limitations: Single-centre retrospective design with relatively short follow-up limits generalisability of findings.
