# Built for UK Clinicians. Not Adapted for Them.

Most clinical evidence tools used by UK doctors were built in the United States. UpToDate was built for US physicians and US insurance workflows. OpenEvidence was built for the US market and has not launched in the UK. ChatGPT for Clinicians launched US-only. Even NEJM Clinician, while available globally, defaults to US clinical context and FDA guidance.

When these products mention NICE, it is usually an afterthought. An addendum. A localisation layer bolted onto an American product.

The Monday Clinical Brief was not localised. It was built here.

## What "UK-Native" Actually Means

**Founded by an NHS consultant.** MCB was created by a palliative medicine consultant working in NHS Wales. The clinical judgment behind every summary comes from someone who works within the NHS, not someone who has read about it.

**NICE and Royal College context is default, not optional.** When a paper challenges or confirms a NICE guideline, the summary says so. When a finding is relevant to a specific Royal College recommendation, you will see it. This context is embedded in the editorial voice, not added as a footnote.

**CPD formatted for GMC revalidation.** MCB digests include reflection prompts, citations, and time tracking designed for the UK appraisal process. Not CME credits for the AMA. Not CE for the ACCME. CPD credits for the GMC.

**Specialty mapping matches UK training.** MCB's 31 specialties map to the UK training pathway — not the American Board structure. Acute Internal Medicine, Palliative Medicine, General Practice with a capital G — these are UK categories serving UK clinicians.

**NHS pricing.** £20 a year. Not $299 with VAT on top. Not "contact sales for institutional pricing." Twenty pounds.

## Why This Matters Now

The clinical AI market is about to get very crowded in the UK. OpenEvidence is planning a UK entry. ChatGPT for Clinicians will likely expand beyond the US. When they arrive, they will bring strong technology and strong brands.

What they will not bring — at least not initially — is:

- Summaries written through an NHS lens.
- CPD output designed for the GMC.
- Pricing that a trainee can afford from their own pocket.
- Understanding of how a Monday morning looks in an NHS hospital.

UK adaptation takes time. Building an FDA-first product and adding NICE guidance later is not the same as building NICE-first.

## The Ad-Free Question

Several of the tools entering the UK market are free because they are ad-supported. Pharmaceutical companies pay to surface their content. Sponsored papers appear alongside unsponsored ones. The curation is shaped, at least in part, by commercial relationships.

MCB has no advertising. No sponsored content. No pharmaceutical partnerships. Every paper in your digest is there because it was published in a top-five journal in your specialty. The only reason it appears is relevance.

At £20 a year, MCB can afford to be ad-free because the economics are simple: a large number of subscribers paying a small amount each. No advertiser subsidy required. No editorial compromise necessary.
