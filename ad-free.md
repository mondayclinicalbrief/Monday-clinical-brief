# Ad-Free Medical Evidence: Why MCB Has No Sponsors

This is a short page about a simple policy. The Monday Clinical Brief does not carry advertising. No pharmaceutical ads. No sponsored papers. No "brought to you by" inserts. No advertiser has any influence over what appears in your digest.

This is not going to change.

## Why We Made This Choice

The clinical evidence space is increasingly funded by pharmaceutical advertising. Several free and low-cost tools for clinicians are supported by sponsored content — papers or recommendations surfaced because a company paid to promote them, not because the evidence warranted attention.

This model has advantages. It keeps tools free for clinicians. It funds platforms that would not exist otherwise. We are not here to condemn it.

But it creates a problem for the reader: **you cannot always tell why a paper is in front of you.** Is it there because it was the most important study published this week? Or because the drug manufacturer paid for placement?

MCB removes that question entirely. Every paper in your digest is there because it was published in a top-five journal in your specialty that week. The inclusion criteria are mechanical: published in a tracked journal, in your specialty, this week. No human or commercial filter decides which papers get promoted.

## How We Afford It

The maths are straightforward. MCB costs £20 per year per subscriber. Production costs are kept low through AI-assisted summarisation with clinical oversight. Distribution is by email — no app store fees, no infrastructure bloat.

At scale, a large number of subscribers paying £20 each sustains the service. No advertiser subsidy is needed. This means no advertiser has leverage over editorial decisions.

The trade-off is that MCB will never be free. But at £20 — less than many doctors spend on coffee in a fortnight — the barrier is low enough that cost should not be the reason you read sponsored content instead.

## Why It Matters for Your CPD

When you present evidence of reading to your appraiser, the source matters. Reading from a pharmaceutical-sponsored digest is not inherently invalid, but it invites questions about independence. Reading from an editorially independent, ad-free source is unambiguous.

Your appraiser wants to see that you are reading for your own professional development, not because an advertiser put something in your path. MCB's ad-free model makes your CPD evidence cleaner.

## What We Are Not Saying

We are not saying that ad-supported tools are unreliable. Many are excellent. Heidi Evidence, QxMD, and others provide genuine clinical value.

We are saying that MCB has made a different choice — one that prioritises editorial independence over free access. At £20 a year, we believe that is the right trade-off for clinicians who want certainty about why they are reading what they are reading.

## The Policy in Full

- MCB does not accept advertising from any source.
- MCB does not accept sponsored content or paid paper placements.
- MCB has no pharmaceutical advisory board or industry-funded editorial board.
- MCB's paper selection is determined solely by journal publication in tracked titles.
- This policy applies to the weekly digest, the blog, the website, and all MCB communications.
